CODING

Rule​s
  1. The event would be held in computer lab. 
  2. Participants have to portray their erudition of coding and debugging. An additional round of multiple-choice quiz will be hosted. 
  3. A group of 2 students can participate for the event. A maximum of 2 teams (2 students X 2) is allowed per college. 
  4. Questions on C, C++, Java, Python, SQL, and HTML can be expected. 
  5. The event would commence on a custom-built software by the organizers.

IT MANAGER

Rule​s
  1. IT manager has 3 rounds and every round has disqualifications. 
  2. Round one, will be held at the computer lab. Quantitative aptitude, English and logical reasoning questions needs to be answered. 
  3. Round two, would be group discussion. Topic for the event will be selected by the judge, at the venue. 
  4. Round three, would be personal interview. 
  5. The event would commence on a custom-built software by the organizers.

    6. Qualification criteria: 

  6. Top 10 performers in Round 1(Aptitude), will qualify to the next round. 
  7. Top 5 performers in Round 2(Group Discussion), will qualify to the final round.
  8. NOTE: The participants of IT manager cannot participate in any other events.

PAPER PRESENTATION

Rule​s
  1. The team gets 4 minutes to present their paper. And 2 minutes will be spent for Q&A. Total [4+2 minutes] is the total time awarded to each team.
  2.  The participants are requested to create a PowerPoint presentation (PPT) to explain their research to the judge.
  3. The organizers reserve the right to change/update the rules of the contest at any point of time and will do their best to inform the participants on the same. However, it is duly requested that the teams are ready for modifications in the rules or timing.
  4.  A team of 2/3 can group together to present their research works.
  5. The paper submitted must be in IEEE format. Plagiarism will be condemned and will lead to disqualification.
